What are lip fillers, how much do they cost, are they safe and did Kylie Jenner have hers removed?

June 2024 · 4 minute read

LIP fillers have risen in popularity over the last few years, with those who want a bigger pout going for the cosmetic procedure.

We have all the info you need to know about getting lip fillers.

What are lip fillers?

Lip fillers have become a popular trend where a cosmetic surgeon injects a dermal filler to plump up your lips.

These types of dermal fillers are sometimes called "hyaluronic acid fillers".

There are a number of different substances used in the injection.

Temporary fillers tend to need redoing every six to nine months depending on your desired look.

Are lip fillers safe?

It is important to do your research before you get lip fillers so that you can find a reputable professional for the job.

Many doctors would argue strongly against getting the procedure at a spa or a beauty salon.

A beautician hasn’t always had the same training as a doctor and they are not able to prescribe medication or treatment if something were to go wrong.

UK legislation on injectables are practically nonexistent so it is imperative that you research your doctor.

Has Kylie Jenner had her lip fillers removed?

Reality star Kylie Jenner has been having regular lip fillers since she was just 15 years old.

However, in 2018, she announced that she had ditched the lip filler.

Confirming the news after an Instagram user commented on her photos, Kylie wrote: "I got rid of all my filler."

Shortly after she started getting filler again.

How long do lip fillers last?

The amount of lip filler needed in a procedure and the length of time it lasts depends on the individual.

The HA fillers typically tend to last between four and six months, but it depends on your desired look.

If you have a faster metabolism the solution gets broken down quicker - so they don't last as long.

How long does the procedure last?

The procedure takes around 15-20 minutes in total.

You can choose the parts of your lip that you want focusing on.

Your doctor can help advise you on the amount of solution needed to suit your facial features.

Are lip fillers sore?

Doctors will apply numbing cream on your lips before the procedure, and people have described the sensation as though someone is pinching your lip.

If done correctly, lip injections don't feel any different from your normal lips.

When do you see the results of lip fillers?

Straight after having fillers, you will notice swelling at first but after 24 hours your lips will be settled and how they will be for the next few months.

The numbing wears off in about 15 minutes and you're able to carry on your day.

What if you don't like your lip fillers?

If you don't like your lip fillers, the good news is they aren't a permanent fixture.

As the filler is a natural occurring substance, it will be broken down over a period of months.

However, if you want them gone immediately there is another injection you can get that dissolves the filler within five minutes and reverses its effects straight away.

Are there many lip filler celeb horror stories?

Sophie Kasaei talked about the true extent of her addiction to lip fillers, which she said left her looking like "the Elephant Man".

The Geordie Shore beauty was warned to stop her addiction by doctors on Channel 5’s Celebrity Botched Up Bodies - as Sophie revealed past treatments had left her in "agony".

Sophie added of one dodgy procedure: “At the time I thought I was big and clever and looked amazing but in reality I just looked like elephant man.”

Sophie's mouth reacted badly to the filler, leaving her lips "horrendous and lumpy".

She added: "I didn’t even know what it was, it could have been anything. Stupid girl."

Sophie and cousin Marnie Simpson have both had fillers to enhance their pouts.

How much do lip fillers cost?

Lip fillers can vary in cost depending where you go to get them done and how much you have.

It can cost between £300 and £1,500 plus.

What can you get done with lip fillers?

You can do both lips, just the bottom, just plump up the top and by as little or as much as you want.

The doctor will advise which complements your features the best.

It is also advised you increase the size gradually over time for more natural results.

What shouldn't you do with lip fillers?

It is recommended to not exercise on the day of your procedure, as this increases blood flow to the face.

It is also advised not to get a massage or facial.

It is important to avoid painkillers, fish oil and Vitamin E, which tend to thin the blood.

Thinner blood will make the area more prone to bruising and bleeding.
