Beach horror as car 'drives off a cliff' with at least one person inside sparking huge emergency res

March 2024 · 2 minute read

A MAJOR rescue operation was launched after a car reportedly plunged off a cliff in Kent.

One person was reported to be inside the vehicle when it went over the edge in Kingsdown, near Deal.

Emergency services, including the air ambulance and lifeboats, charged to the scene just before 3pm on Sunday, KentOnline reports.

It was confirmed on Tuesday the driver of the car, a man in his 50s, died at the scene - he was the only person in the vehicle.

The exact details of how the car plunged down the rocks are unclear.

A police spokesman told KentOnline: "Kent Police was called to a report of a single vehicle collision at 2.45pm on Sunday.

"Officers along with other emergency services attended the Kingsdown beach area of Deal. The occupant of the vehicle, a man in his fifties, was confirmed deceased.

"The death is not being treated as suspicious and a report will be prepared for the coroner."

A Kent Fire and Rescue spokesperson told The Sun on Sunday: "I can confirm KFRS was called to an incident in that area at 2.49pm by paramedics from South East Coast Ambulance Service.

"Three fire engines and fire officers attended, and assistance was rendered as required.
