The 9 Best Lip Masks, Tested and Reviewed

April 2024 · 17 minute read

Regardless of the state of your lips (dry! chapped! devoid of human contact!), a lip mask can take a tired pout and transform it into a hydrated pair of lips. Celebrities like Chrissy Teigen use this self-care hack frequently as a way to rejuvenate the place on your face that is over-talked and underappreciated. In addition to hydration, dermatologic surgeon Mary L. Stevenson, MD, tells PEOPLE that lip masks can "repair and prevent skin damage from UV [rays]" which can be "essential" especially during the summer.

Working with 25 different lip masks, our team had an in-office spa day, and applied a wealth of creamy, glossy, and nourishing masks to our lips. Testing for how the masks felt on the skin and the overall hydration and look after use, we crafted a list of our favorite lip masks, keeping in mind different needs like sensitive skin and duration of use. Then we continued testing these lip masks at home for six months and thus, created our best list.

Read on for the lip masks PEOPLE considers the best for hydration, look, and overall feel.

Things to Consider Before Buying Lip Masks

Ingredients

The ingredients in the lip mask will determine what effects (and results) you see after use, so you have to pay close attention to what you're spreading on your lips. To start, you should look for ingredients that moisturize. "These include humectants — which bring water in, like a sponge — and occlusive ingredients like petrolatum, [which] seals in the moisture the humectant brought in," says Dr. Stevenson.

She adds that other ingredients like "antioxidants and peptides" can repair damage from and protect against environmental factors like UV rays. "[You] also might want to look for gentle exfoliants, but [may not] want to do this if your lips are sensitive," she says.

When to Apply

Many lip masks are designed to be worn overnight so they can work their magic for a longer period of time, but some are intended for single use and can be wiped off after a few minutes. You should consider both types of masks depending on the intensity of hydration and long-term results you're looking for. For perpetually dry lips, perhaps consider wearing overnight masks — like the SHE LOVES 2PCS Lip Mask Overnight — regularly to maintain moisture.

Lip Masks vs. Lip Balms

One thing that might get confusing when looking for a lip mask is its similarity to a lip balm. Though similar in function (to hydrate), lip masks are for long-term use — generally overnight or applied well before you put on makeup — whereas lip balms are lighter and can be applied on top of lipstick for extra hydration throughout the day. Just be sure to check the labels and instructions when buying both to make sure you're getting the product you need.

FAQs

Do lip masks really work?

Yes, lip masks are loaded with nourishing ingredients that hydrate and moisturize lips. They are great for counteracting the drying effect of wearing lipstick and sun exposure. And if you have varying needs, there are lip masks for that too — such as for those with sensitive skin: Dermelect Smooth & Supple Lip Mask Melt. Or perhaps you'd like a little plumping effect as well — you can try the Averr Aglow Goodnight Kiss Lip Mask.

Can you leave a lip mask on overnight?

Many lip masks are intended to be worn overnight. Look at your particular product for guidance as some lip masks are made to be used for just a few minutes, while others are labeled for overnight use. You can also check the product instructions to see how often you should wear the lip masks at all — but most lip masks can be used several times a week, or even daily if your lips are especially dry.

What is the difference between a lip mask and a lip balm?

Usually worn for longer stretches of time, lip masks provide a more long-lasting effect than lip balms, which absorb into your skin quickly. Lip masks are more hydrating — often due to ingredients like hyaluronic acid — and sometimes contain exfoliants that are not found in lip balm.

Do lip masks work on chapped lips?

They sure do! Lip masks contain a bevy of oils and butters that hydrate and moisturize the driest and most chapped lips. If you're really needing a surge of moisture and want to lock in that hydration, try a thicker lip mask like the Sara Happ The Dream Slip, which works well on cracked and chapped lips.
