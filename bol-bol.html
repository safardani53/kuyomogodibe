Bol Bol | Early Life, & Parents

Bol Manute Bol, professionally Bol Bol, was born on November 16, 1999, in Khartoum, Sudan, to parents Manute Bol and Ajok Deng. He was named after his late great-grandfather and Dinka chief Bol Chol Bol.

His father, Manute, was a professional basketball player and political activist and was considered among the best shot-blockers in the sport’s history.

Likewise, he was also the only NBA player to retire with more career blocked shots than points scored. 

Moving on, Manute was accused of being a spy and was barred by the Sudanese government from fleeing to the US after a missile from the American army struck the country’s north in 1998. 

He and his family traveled to Cairo, Egypt, in 2001, where they got grounded for many months due to visa problems, although they already had tickets to the States. 

Later that same year, when Bol was two years old, his family flew to Connecticut on the American East Coast as designated political refugees.

But, despite his father’s status, Bol’s initial years were not the easiest, leading to an incident that changed the course of his life.

Further

He started playing basketball at four upon encouragement from his father, although he was initially interested in playing football.

When asked about his childhood memory, Bol said, ” I remember basketball; at first, it wasn’t my thing. I was crazy about football, and my dad forced me to play basketball a bit. He came to see me one day and said, “I know how you feel because even when I was your age, I wanted to kick a ball. But my family, given my size, quickly dissuaded me.” He was right!”

After that, he eventually began training with his father in the gym. And at age seven, he moved to Olathe, Kansas, a city with a large South Sudanese population, to begin his career.

Likewise, in his seventh grade, Bol got featured in a highlight video at an Indianapolis basketball camp, which caught a lot of attention, including CBS Sports and The Washington Post. 

He said, “I truly fell in love with basketball. When I was in the seventh grade, after my first basketball mixtape went viral, it was for the first time the world same me playing. I just got a lot of positive feedback. It made me like basketball even more.”

Similarly, when Bol was in the eighth grade, he got his first NCAA Division I offer from New Mexico State.

Other than that, he is American by nationality and Sudanese by ethnicity.

Bol Bol | High School & College Career

As for Bol’s education, he attended Blue Valley Northwest High School. He played freshman basketball for the junior varsity team. 

But later, as Bol did not live in his school district, he transferred to Bishop Miege High School in Roeland Park, Kansas, and continued his freshman season.

While there, the NBA player made his debut for the Bishop junior varsity team on December 11, 2014, and displayed his shot-blocking and shooting ability.

In May, he participated in the Under-15 Amateur Athletic Union (AAU) team KC Run GMC at the Jayhawk Invitational in Kansas City, Missouri, and scored 15 points in one game. 

By then, the basketball player had started receiving interest from several NCAA Division I programs, including Kansas and Oklahoma.

Likewise, in his sophomore season, Bol got ranked as one of the top players in the class of 2018 by recruiting service 247Sports.

He transferred from Bishop Miege to Mater Dei High School in Santa Ana, California, for his junior year in November 2016.

After completing his junior season, in 2017, Bol transferred to Findlay Prep in Henderson, Nevada, and finished his senior season earning All-USA Boys Basketball Second-Team honors from USA Today High School Sports.

Similarly, he was also selected for the 2018 McDonald’s All-American Boys Game but could not play due to his injury.

Due to Bol’s successful high school season and outstanding playing skills, he received many offers from Arizona, USC, Oregon, Kentucky, and UCLA, to play college basketball.

University Career 

Bol chose Oregon State University and started his basketball career there. After joining as a freshman in the 2018–19 season, he entered the season being one of the best prospects for the 2019 NBA Draft. 

During one of his games against San Diego, Bol injured his left foot, ending his season.

So, he only decided to go for the NBA Draft after his freshman season.

Bol Bol | NBA Career

The Miami Heat drafted Bol Bol 44th overall pick in the 2019 NBA Draft. Initially, he was the top 5-10 pick but got slid in the draft until the second round.

Later, Bol was traded to the Denver Nuggets as he would take a long time to play at the NBA level and had just recovered from his foot surgery. 

But again, the Nuggets sent him to the Windy City Bulls. There he got a chance to improvise himself and found a rhythm. Sadly, COVID-19 hit, and the Nuggets didn’t play from March 11 until July 1. 

On September 6, Bol signed a two-way contract with the Nuggets and split time with the Windy City Bulls.

Moreover, he made his first double-double in the NBA G League on November 20 against the Fort Wayne Mad Ants.

Bol made his NBA debut in the 2020 NBA Bubble on August 1, 2020, recording 5 points and four rebounds in under 11 minutes of play in a 125-105 loss to the Miami Heat. 

2022

He signed with the Detroit Pistons on January 10, 2022, but the signing wasn’t completed as he failed his physical fitness exam.

Later, he was traded to Boston Celtics on January 19, 2022. 

Bol signed to Orlando Magic alongside a future second-round draft pick and cash considerations. 

The NBA player recorded a career-high 26 points, 12 rebounds, and three blocks in a 126-109 loss against the Minnesota Timberwolves.
