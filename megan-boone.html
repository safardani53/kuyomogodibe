Megan Boone Biography

Megan Boone was born on April 29, 1983, and she is 40 years old. Her zodiac sign is Taurus, and she was born in Petoskey, Michigan. She grew up in The Villages, Florida, after her parents relocated there as a child to be closer to her grandparents.

Her father’s name is Marcus Whitney Boone, and her mother’s name is Jennifer Parr. Her mother is the Sales Director. Similarly, her grandfather was the developer of The Villages, H. Gary Morse. Furthermore, Boone’s ancestors are English and German, with some Dutch and Scottish thrown in for good measure.

Megan Boone Education

When it comes to her educational background, this personality studied acting as a student. She went to Belleview High School and graduated in 2001. Boone then earned a Bachelor of Fine Arts (BFA) in Acting from Florida State University’s School of Theatre in 2005. The actress then enrolled at Bard College in late 2017 to pursue an MBA in Sustainability.

Career

Megan Boone is an accomplished actress. Boone is best known for her role as FBI agent and profiler Elizabeth Keen on The Blacklist. Similarly, this actress appeared in an episode of Blue Bloods and had a recurring role in Law & Order: LA. Boone has also appeared in films such as My Bloody Valentine 3D in 2009 and Step Up Revolution in 2012.

When asked about the start of her career, the actress revealed that she was hooked on acting at the age of seven. This occurred after her grandparents took Boone to see a Broadway play starring Nathan Lane.

Furthermore, this individual attended the Asolo Repertory Theatre with Jane Alexander and Edwin Sherin. Similarly, Boone worked on a play with playwright Mark Medoff. This personality also credits Jane Alexander with inspiring her to continue acting after she considered quitting earlier in her career.

This character also appeared in Limonade Tous Les Jours. The play by Charles L. Mee makes its Los Angeles debut in 2007. She was also nominated for two LA Weekly Theater Awards for her performance in this play. Following that, she made her feature film debut in My Bloody Valentine 3D. It was released in 2009 as a horror film. She then went on to play a supporting role in Sex and the City 2 in 2010.

Boone portrayed Junior Deputy District Attorney Lauren Stanton in Law & Order: LA in 2010. The actress made her directorial debut with Eggshells for Soil the same year. It’s an independent film with some scenes shot in her hometown of The Villages, Florida. Similarly, in 2012, the actress appeared in Step Up Revolution. It is the fourth installment in the Step Up series.

The actress then starred in the 2012 independent drama Leave Me Like You Found Me. Boone won the Gen Art Film Festival Award for Best Actress for this role. Similarly, in 2013, this actress had a recurring role on Blue Bloods. The same year, she starred in The Blacklist as FBI agent Elizabeth Keen. Recently, actress Boone and her production company, Weird Sister, signed a first-look deal with Sony Pictures Television.
