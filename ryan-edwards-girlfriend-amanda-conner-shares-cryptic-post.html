Ryan Edwards girlfriend Amanda Conner shares cryptic post about 'prayers' after Teen Mom star lan

May 2024 · 4 minute read

TEEN Mom's Ryan Edwards' girlfriend Amanda Conner has asked Teen Mom fans for their support after Ryan ends up in jail.

Amanda reshared an uplifting video that spotlighted her faith in her recent Instagram Stories timeline.

The clip that Amanda, 33, reposted was a montage video that had a few Bible verses in it.

Synced over the video was audio of Family Feud host Steve Harvey, 66, encouraging people to pray and strengthen their relationship with God.

Accompanying each sentence of what Steve said was a quote from the Good Book.

"Don't forget to pray," Steve said.

He added that people shouldn't be "ashamed to pray or too proud to pray" either.

Steve explained that "prayer changes things."

Amanda appears to be turning to her Christian faith to help her and Ryan get through their troublesome time.

The Teen Mom OG alum was recently sentenced to one month in prison after he failed to follow the terms of his probation.

THE COUNTDOWN

Amanda shared her feelings, as well as a "countdown" to the MTV star's release, with a social media post.

She posted a photo of herself riding with Ryan, 35, on the back of his motorcycle.

In the snapshot, both she and the TV personality were shown wearing helmets and hoodies as they rode atop the sports bike.

Text over the photo read: "I miss you dearly. I don't care what they say about you or me, you have been doing so good and I just want to say I'm proud of you."

Amanda continued: "I love you," before adding, "39 day count down baby."

Ryan was arrested during a December 5 court hearing after District Attorney Coty Wamp told Judge Starnes that he failed to follow the terms of his plea deal. 

Ryan arrived back in court from Silverdale Detention Center on December 8 to get the results of his drug test in his driving under the influence, possession of a controlled substance, and simple possession court case.  

BREAKIN' ALL THE RULES

The U.S. Sun can exclusively reveal Ryan passed his drug test but was still given over a month in jail for not following the terms of his probation.

Ryan was led into the courthouse by an officer, as he was handcuffed and in an orange Hamilton County Sheriff’s Office jumpsuit. 

District Attorney Coty Wamp began the hearing: “We have a passed drug screen. The state is surprised.

"The state believes Mr. Edwards doesn’t want to take his probation seriously. It’s good he passed a drug screen."

The DA continued: “He needs the Vivitrol shot. He was a couple of weeks late on it. My request would be we monitor his progress and ask the court to submit the report on his progress, including his AA meetings. 

“All of that should be simple for someone on a rehabilitation track to do well and doesn’t want to go to jail for years.”

Ryan’s lawyer responded: “He passed his drug screening. He should’ve been more diligent with the court’s requirements.

The attorney then reasoned that the "ultimate objective" of the requirements is that he doesn’t use drugs.

They then added: "And he hasn’t used.”

Judge Starnes fired back: “There was plenty of time for him to get it out of his system from the time took the test." 

Ryan's counsel then claimed he had the shot scheduled for December 12. 

Judge Starnes said: “I don't know how many more opportunities Mr. Edwards is entitled to.

"He has failed to do what he’s supposed to. He’s been revoked and put in jail for almost three months. He’s had violations at Oasis and CADAS, but they didn’t kick him out."

An intense back and forth commenced between the judge, Ryan's lawyer, and Ryan himself.

It ended with Ryan telling the judge: "I am sorry. I will do what I am supposed to do," and also pleading for another opportunity which the judge seemed hesitant to give.

The judge ultimately resigned to hold off his judgment and move the hearing to January 17.

Ryan will remain incarcerated until the scheduled hearing.
