The Best World Book Day Costume Ideas 2023 UK

September 2024 · 6 minute read

WORLD BOOK DAY falls on Thursday 2 March this year, and kids are always as excited about getting dressed up for the occasion.

The best World Book Day costume ideas are fun, easy for parents to put together and comfortable enough for a child to wear all day in school.

The good news is, you don't have to spend all weekend non-stop crafting to create a brilliant World Book Day outfit.

For toddlers, we'd recommend a soft or plush costume: from Peppa Pig to the Very Hungry Caterpillar, your little one will look adorable. And age 3 or 4 is probably your last chance to dress your son up as a Gruffalo, so make the most of it.

Older school kids tend to want to put an individual touch on their World Book Day costumes, but certain authors are always a good bet: Dr. Seuss, Roald Dahl and David Walliams have plenty to choose from, and Harry Potter is a perennial favourite.

If you haven't sorted a 2023 World Book Day costume, don't panic, most of our recommendations offer next day delivery.

And of course World Book Day isn't just about dressing up - don't forget to use the £1 token from school to get a new book for your child.

When is World Book Day 2023?

World Book Day falls on Thursday 2 March and the emphasis is on making it YOUR World Book Day

The international day has been held on the same day each year since it began in 1995.

What to dress up as for World Book Day

The idea is to dress up as a character from your favourite book and popular choices for World Book day are characters from books by Julia Donaldson, Roald Dahl and Dr Seuss.

Unless your child's school or nursery has strict rules, it's usually fairly relaxed and many kids just see it as a fun opportunity to where fancy dress.

The most popular children's book characters for World Book Day include Peter Rabbit, the BFG and  - of course - Harry Potter.

Where to buy World Book Day costumes

The majority of fancy dress and party stores will be stocking World Book Day costumes as it's become a big event for school-age kids in the UK.

Online, Amazon always a has a great collection - that you can get in a hurry if needed.

Plus, check out supermarket clothing departments such as Sainsbury's TU brand and George at ASDA - plus high street shops such as H&M, Argos and John Lewis.

Also, online fancy dress suppliers such as Party Delights, Fancydress.com and Smiffys will have a good selection to choose from.
