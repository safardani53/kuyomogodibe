Apple iPhone 7 and 7plus launches and here's how to get one delivered to your door by tomorrow morni

July 2024 · 2 minute read

YOU could be holding a shiny new iPhone by tomorrow even if you can't get to the shops in time for today's launch.

Fans have been patiently waiting to get their hands on the new iPhone 7 and iPhone 7 Plus devices, which come in a retro red colour.

Eager beavers who wanted to get their hands on the new devices were met with a blank screen and the words "we've got something special in store for you".

Apple told fans that it "can't wait for you to see it" but to return to the site later.

Customers can choose from jet black, black, silver, rose gold and gold in a metallic finish.

And if you order before 7pm you can expect to receive it by tomorrow, it said on the website.

The iPhone 7 costs from £599 with 128GB of memory and the 7 Plus from £719 for the same storage.

For those looking to trade-in an iPhone 6, Carphone Warehouse is offering to up to £160, dependent on size and condition – terms and conditions apply.

Apple said the new phones come with the “best battery life ever” and are water resistant.

The brand new red iPhone is also on sale, for £100 more for the 7 and 7 Plus.

A small donation of the proceeds will go toward providing access to life-saving ARV medication in sub-Saharan Africa, through charity RED.

The announcement came as a shock to industry experts, who were expecting the rumoured new iPhone 7 to launch at Apple's annual Spring event.

It's also announced a brand new iPad which is cheaper than the iPad Air 2.

The new tablet comes with a 9.7" display and 10-hours battery life with up to 128GB storage.
