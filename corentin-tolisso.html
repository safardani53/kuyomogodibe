Corentin Tolisso Career

  • Corentin Tolisso joined Lyon at the age of 13. In there, he made his first professional debut with a 4-0 victory over Club Nice in Ligue 1. On October 24, 2013, he made his European club competition debut, winning 1-0 against HNK Rijeka in the group stage of the “2013-14 UEFA Europa League.” After a week, Tolisso signed his first professional contract with Lyon until 2017. On March 9, 2014, he scored his first goal for the Club in a 2-1 victory over the Club Bordeaux. He appeared in 146 matches for his Club, scoring 22 goals.
  • On June 14, 2017, he signed a 5-year contract with Bayern Munich for $41.5 million plus a potential bonus of up to $7.46 million. On August 5, he made his first appearance for this Club in a penalty shoot-out game against Borussia Dortmund, which this Club won. He appeared in 17 games and scored two goals for his hometown club.
  • Tolisso was born in France and has represented the country at various youth levels, including captaining the U21 side, but he was also eligible to represent Togo due to his paternal lineage. Togo manager Claude Le Roy revealed in 2016 that he would try to persuade Tolisso to represent the country rather than France. Tolisso had previously stated that he had ties to Togo but was loyal to France because “I was born here and grew up here.”
  • Tolisso, who had previously represented France at various youth levels, was called up to the senior squad for the first time in March 2017 to face Luxembourg and Spain. He made his debut against the latter on March 28, 2017, starting the game and being replaced by Thomas Lemar in the 80th minute of a 2–0 friendly home loss.
