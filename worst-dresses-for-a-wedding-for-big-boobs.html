I'm a fashion whizz &amp; these are the worst wedding guest dresses for big boobs - wrap dresses are a h

May 2024 · 3 minute read

IF you have a bigger bust you know all too well how difficult it can be to find the perfect outfit. 

But when you’re looking for the right look for a wedding it can be even harder. 

Being pigeon holed into occasion wear means you can’t rely on an oversized T-shirt or wear your Saturday night corset dress that might reveal a little too much for the family's Grandma's liking.

And that's not to mention the fashion mishaps you could get yourself into.

Throwing up confetti energetically, dancing, and even standing still holding your clutch bag under arm, drink in one hand and canape in another is no mean feat when you have a big bust. 

But there are some dresses you can avoid to ensure you don’t have to worry about stealing attention from the bride and more importantly, feel comfortable all day.

Here, Fabulous’ Fashion Editor Clemmie Fieldsend reveals what you shouldn’t wear to a wedding if you have big boobs.

WRAP DRESS

Wrap dresses are brilliant at showing off or creating a waist, even when you don't have one.

But when it comes to busty ladies they’re a no-go, sorry.

There's always the chance of the tie at the side coming loose, unbeknownst to you, and your boobs are the problem.

Most read in Fabulous

The fuller your chest the harder the fabric has to work and is clinging on for dear life to stay tied and in place. 

To prevent this sew (of glue) on clothing poppers to fasten the dress in place, guaranteeing no big reveals.

You can get them for as little as £2 on Amazon. 

RACER DRESS

Racer style tops and frocks are loved by Kim Kardashian and Zara McDermott, but for ladies with big chests they can cause nip-slips. 

The cut away neckline and back means if you wear a bra your straps are on show, and if you go without you are giving your boobs free reign to escape out of the sides. 

If you can’t resist this trend, invest in a supportive and sturdy stick on bra. 

STRAPLESS

Whether you're in a bar or at a wedding, you will have seen a woman who is constantly pulling and shimmying in her strapless dress. 

If you have big boobs they’re just a constant pain.

Unless you’re like model Ashley Graham who has a seamstress on hand to make sure there is no movement at all on the dress, give this a miss. 

You don’t want to spend all day and night hoiking your dress up, and getting it dirty around the top in the process, in fear of it falling down. 

CUT OUT 

Cut out style dresses under or in the middle of the bust are a recipe for disaster.

Whilst they look sexy and are a favourite with the likes of Emily Ratajkowski and Dua Lipa, there are just too many avenues for your boobs to escape from, leaving you constantly checking all day to see if you’ve had a fashion mishap. 

But you can opt for smaller, more demure cut outs elsewhere, for example on the waist or hip.
