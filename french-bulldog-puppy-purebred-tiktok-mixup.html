I bought a 3k purebred French bulldog puppy I was baffled when it grew up to be something tota

July 2024 · 3 minute read

A WOMAN was left shocked after her puppy worth thousands of pounds grew up to be a different breed than she paid for.

Lisa posted the shocking before and after of her pet, which she thought was a French Bulldog.

The video on TikTok shows a young puppy with its eyes closed, with the caption: "When your "French Bulldog" you paid £3,000 for turns into..."

The next photo shows Lisa's daughter posing with the canine, who grew up to be less of a French Bulldog and more of a regular one.

The cute puppy turned into a rather awkward-looking adult dog, with misshapen ears and a cheeky smile.

The dog's appearance is a far cry from what Lisa had hoped for - a smaller, squish faced dog.

Social media users were quick to comment on the unfortunate mix-up.

One said: "Looks like an old time bulldog! Still gorgeous."

Lisa responded: "Yeah, think there was a bit of cross breeding maybe accidentally. But wouldn't change him for the world. Best wee (big) dog."

Other followers pointed out the benefits of not having a Frenchie, which are known for being hard to maintain.

Another commenter said: "Probably save as much on vet bills to be honest."

According to the pet advice site French Bull Dog Owner, the purebred pups cost an average of £1,700 per year in vet bills and other expenses.

Others were quick to point out Lisa's misfortune in paying £3,000 for her less than purebred dog.

One user said: "A well bred pedigree from a reputable breeder shouldn't cost any more than £1,800. Y'all need to do better research when looking for breeders."

Last year, a mum who thought she was buying a purebred French bulldog also had a shocking surprise.

Bethan Cupples, 21, found the adorable puppy on Gumtree for only £600, and took home her new five-week-old pal.

But when canine Luna began growing, she was not the short-haired, flat nosed purebred Bethany had hoped for.

She took little Luna to the local veterinarian, where she found out her beloved pet was actually a mixture of a Yorkshire Terrier and French bulldog.

Bethany even told Ladbible: "We did look for a Frenchie, but as soon as we laid eyes on her we instantly fell in love.
