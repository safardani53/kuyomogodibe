College Career

In the Bruins’ 2005–06 season, he was a reserve to Jordan Farmar, becoming the next season’s starting point guard.

In December 2006, he was awarded the MVP of the Maui Invitational Tournament, and on the 4th of December, 2006, and again on February 18, 2007, he was named the Pacific-10 Conference Player of the Week.

Collison recorded 2.2 steals per game during the 2006-07 season, the most in the Pacific-10 Conference. Darren also averaged 5.7 assists (2nd in the Pac-10) and a 3-point field goal percentage of 444.

For his junior and senior years, Collison returned to UCLA and ended up appearing in 142 games at UCLA, tied for the most ever.

He was selected to the All-Pac-10 team in his senior year in 2008–09 after scoring 4.7 assists,14.4 points, and 1.6 steals per game.

He took the league in free throw percentage and was seventh in assists, steals, and assists-to-turnover ratio.

That year, Collison received the Frances Pomeroy Naismith Trophy, awarded to the best player in college with 6 ft 0 in (1.83 m) or shorter. He was also named co-Most Valuable Player (MVP) of the Bruins with Josh Shipp.
