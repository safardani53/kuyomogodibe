What is the best home brewing kit for beginners?

The best home brewing kits for beginners are ones that include everything you need to get started - that means the equipment as well as the ingredients - although these can cost a little more. Most kits don’t come with bottles and caps or kegs for you to store your beer though, so that’s worth bearing in mind. If you’re looking to build your own kit, the key piece of equipment you need is the fermenting bucket and one that’s fitted with an airlock - to let the CO2 out - will be the easiest to use and will keep your beer safe from bugs and bacteria. Some fermenting buckets have a tap at the bottom for bottling but if not, you’ll need a siphon to help you draw the beer out once it’s ready. You’ll also need a stainless steel paddle or stirrer with a long handle to help you mix the ingredients for brewing. And of course to keep your kit clean, you’ll need chemicals to sterilise it before use. To make the beer itself, you’ll also need the basic ingredients, which will depend on the type of brew you’re going for. Some recipes will also require additional equipment, such as a sieve, but a great recipe should tell you everything you need.

Where do you buy home brew kits?

Pre-assembled home brew kits and any extra equipment you might need are widely available online. There are specialist shops like Love Brewing that sell courses as well as different home brewing kits to get you started. Wilko is a good place to find budget-friendly ingredients as well as essential equipment. And if you’re looking for something more specialist, you could try searching on Amazon or even eBay.

Which home brew kit is the best?

There is no best home brew kit as such. Instead, it depends on your preferences and how experienced you are at making beer.
